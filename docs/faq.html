General
Radiology dictation software is a specialized voice recognition tool designed specifically for radiologists. Unlike generic speech-to-text tools, it understands radiology-specific medical terminology, imaging modalities (CT, MRI, X-Ray, Ultrasound, PET-CT), anatomical nomenclature, and clinical phrasing.

PISUM's built-in radiology dictation engine — Sally AI — goes a step further: it processes audio in real time via a secure cloud API (audio is never stored, only the text transcription is returned), supports 23 languages with radiology-specific medical vocabulary, and dictates directly into structured report fields. The result is a formatted, exportable report — no post-editing, no per-minute voice fees. Patient reports always stay on your local machine.
Yes — when used correctly as a productivity layer, not a replacement for clinical judgment. AI in radiology reporting can dramatically reduce the time spent writing repetitive boilerplate, eliminate copy-paste errors across reports, enforce consistent medical terminology, and significantly reduce radiologist burnout.

Research shows radiologists spend up to 40% of their shift on report writing. AI-assisted radiology workflow software like PISUM can reduce this by 60–80%, bringing average report time to under 60 seconds. PISUM's approach: AI structures and transcribes, the radiologist validates and signs. AI features use a secure cloud API — audio is never stored, and patient reports never leave your local machine. The radiologist stays in control; the AI removes the friction.

Features
Sally AI is PISUM's voice recognition engine. It converts your speech into structured radiology report text in real time via a secure cloud API — audio is never stored. It is optimized for medical vocabulary across 23 supported languages.
PISUM ships with 112 structured templates covering all major imaging modalities (CT, MRI, X-Ray, Ultrasound, PET-CT…). You can also create and save unlimited custom templates — either from scratch or by modifying existing ones. Custom templates are stored locally and travel with your user profile.
PISUM exports to PDF and Microsoft Word (.docx). Reports can be formatted with custom headers, footers, logos, and signatures. Bulk export for batch reporting is available on the Clinic plan. Direct RIS/PACS integration is on our roadmap.
