02 Sub-processors & third-party transfers

PISUM uses two third-party sub-processors. In accordance with GDPR Art. 28 and Art. 13(1)(e), we disclose them in full below.

Sub-processor Country Data processed Legal basis for transfer Safeguard
Supabase, Inc. EU (Ireland / eu-west-1) Account data, authentication, license GDPR Art. 6(1)(b) — no transfer outside EEA EU hosting — no SCC required
Deepgram, Inc. USA (San Francisco, CA) Real-time voice audio stream (no patient identifiers unless dictated) GDPR Art. 49(1)(a) — explicit consent SCC 2021/914 + explicit consent dialog
⚠ Deepgram benchmarking risk — disclosed. According to Deepgram's data processing policy, Deepgram may share audio recordings with third-party speech recognition providers ("industry peers" / competitors) for model benchmarking and evaluation purposes.

Your options:
1. Opt out of Deepgram's Model Improvement Partnership Program (contact security@deepgram.com).
2. Request that PISUM sign a DPA with Deepgram that explicitly limits processing to core service delivery only.
3. Use manual text entry — no data is transmitted to any third party.

This risk is disclosed in PISUM's in-app consent dialog (Art. 49(1)(a)) before voice dictation is first activated. You may revoke consent at any time from Settings → Privacy.
No other sub-processors. PISUM does not use any analytics service (no Mixpanel, Amplitude, Segment, etc.), advertising network, or third-party cloud that accesses patient health data. AI text enhancement (Gemini) operates on report text that you explicitly submit — it does not receive patient identifiers automatically.

03 Secure architecture

🔒 Patient data — local only

Radiology reports, patient data, and medical images are stored exclusively in a local SQLite database on your workstation. The database is encrypted with AES-256-GCM at rest. PISUM does not require an Internet connection for data entry, report editing, or export (PDF, DICOM SR). Only optional AI features require network access.

🔑 Authentication & session security

Authentication tokens are issued by Supabase (EU) and stored locally using Fernet symmetric encryption (AES-128-CBC + HMAC-SHA256). Sessions are automatically revoked on application exit. Role-based access control (admin, radiologist, technician, secretary) is enforced at the application layer.

🎙️ Voice dictation — Deepgram Nova-2 Medical

When voice dictation is activated (and only after explicit GDPR Art. 49(1)(a) consent), PISUM streams real-time audio to Deepgram, Inc. using the Nova-2 Medical model. Audio is not stored by Deepgram after transcription. Only the resulting text is returned to your local machine. No patient identifiers (name, date of birth, file number) are included in the audio stream unless you dictate them explicitly.

Consent is mandatory before first use. Voice dictation is disabled by default. A dedicated GDPR Art. 49(1)(a) consent dialog — disclosing the Deepgram benchmarking risk — must be accepted before the microphone is ever activated. You can revoke this consent independently at any time from Settings → Privacy.

04 Sovereignty and telemetry

🛡️ Health data sovereignty

Since the core application runs entirely on the user's workstation, patient health data (reports, images, identifiers) is never processed by any cloud sub-processor. Legal archiving and long-term storage remain the sole responsibility of the institution's RIS/PACS system, ensuring full data sovereignty over medical records.

Voice audio is the only health-adjacent data transmitted outside your machine, and only when dictation is explicitly enabled and consented to (see Section 2 above).

📝 Local application logs

PISUM generates local technical error logs to ensure software stability. These logs are strictly programmed to capture no business or health data:

  • No patient names, dates of birth, or identification numbers.
  • No medical terms, report content, or voice transcriptions.
  • Only system error codes, loading times, and UI interaction events.

📋 Incident log (GDPR Art. 33)

PISUM maintains a local encrypted incident log for Art. 33 compliance. Any security event (unauthorized access attempt, encryption failure, abnormal session activity) is automatically recorded with timestamp, severity, and scope. Incidents meeting the "risk to natural persons" threshold are reported to the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours.

05 Patient rights (Art. 15–22)

The GDPR guarantees patients the right of access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, and objection regarding their personal data.

Rights regarding account data (PISUM users)

Users may exercise the following rights against PISUM at any time by contacting MyPisum@Proton.me:

  • Right of access (Art. 15) — obtain a copy of your account data held by PISUM / Supabase.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17) — request deletion of your account and all associated data. Processed within 30 days.
  • Right to portability (Art. 20) — receive your account data in a structured, machine-readable format (JSON).
  • Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7(3)) — revoke Deepgram voice dictation consent at any time from Settings → Privacy, without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
  • Right to object (Art. 21) — object to processing based on legitimate interest.

Rights regarding patient health data

Patient records are held locally by your institution. PISUM does not retain patient data after the report is exported. Patients wishing to exercise their GDPR rights (access, erasure, portability) must contact the Data Controller of the clinic or hospital — typically the institution's DPO — who will act directly on the RIS/PACS or Hospital Information System (HIS) where final reports are archived.

07 Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA · Art. 35)

PISUM has conducted a formal Data Protection Impact Assessment as required by GDPR Art. 35, given that the software processes special category health data (Art. 9) on a large scale.

The DPIA covers three processing activities:

  • Account & subscription data — Supabase (EU). Risk: Low. Mitigated by EU hosting, SCC, encryption.
  • Voice dictation audio — Deepgram (USA). Risk: Medium-High. Mitigated by explicit consent, audio deletion after transcription, opt-out options.
  • Local patient database — SQLite on workstation. Risk: Medium. Mitigated by AES-256-GCM encryption, role-based access, secure delete, audit log.
Full DPIA document available. The complete Data Protection Impact Assessment, including risk matrices, mitigation measures, and DPO sign-off, is available at pisum.app/dpia.html.
